## Summary
Rather than treating work as a single fixed effort-to-impact point, Cutler argues teams should map the full effort-vs-value curve (sigmoid, decay, linear, step, J-curve, delayed step) before prioritizing initiatives. For data leaders, this framing is critical when justifying investments like data platform migrations or governance programs, which often follow delayed-step or J-curve patterns where value is invisible until completion—making early alignment on expected curve shape essential for stakeholder trust and resource continuity.

## Key Excerpts
> Prioritization should focus on a high-level focus area and its value curve over time, not just individual features or tasks evaluated at a single effort point.
> Curves A, E, and F can look nearly identical at the start, yet require radically different organizational preparation, risk tolerance, and leading indicators to manage successfully.
> Some effort-value curves offer natural exit points with low sunk cost, while others demand that teams surface and test core assumptions as early as possible to avoid compounding risk.
