## Summary
Tristan Handy argues that AI's near-term impact on core analytics priorities—data quality, observability, platform spend, and trust—remains limited due to fundamental architectural constraints in current LLMs, particularly their inability to reason through causal chains. However, he identifies metadata as the critical bridge between structured data and AI, positioning dbt as the foundational source of truth that will enable meaningful AI integration for data teams. For data leaders, this reframes AI investment strategy: rather than forcing AI onto current pain points, the priority should be building robust metadata infrastructure that will unlock future AI capabilities.

## Key Excerpts
> Current enterprise data priorities—code quality, observability, vendor consolidation, and data trust—have little meaningful overlap with today's AI tooling capabilities.
> Transformer-based LLMs excel at language tasks because language is already compressed information, but struggle with causal reasoning and real-world physics simulation.
> The integration of structured data and AI will ultimately be driven by metadata, making dbt's role as a metadata source of truth central to any AI strategy.
