## Summary
The article argues that data, like sports broadcasts, is never consumed raw — it is always framed by narrative commentary that shapes interpretation more powerfully than the underlying numbers themselves. This matters for data leaders because the words analysts choose around data (not just the data itself) functionally determine business decisions, making editorial judgment a core governance concern. As AI systems increasingly serve as the intermediary layer interpreting and presenting data, the "invisible hand" shaping organizational understanding shifts to an inscrutable, automated narrator.

## Key Excerpts
> Commentary surrounding data shapes interpretation more than the numbers themselves; audiences decode data through the framing they're given, not through independent analysis.
> Analysts and data communicators exercise quiet editorial power — choosing what to highlight, downplay, or ignore — in ways that anchor downstream business opinions.
> As AI takes over data narration via tools like MCP servers, the framing layer becomes automated and opaque, raising urgent questions about who controls interpretive bias.
