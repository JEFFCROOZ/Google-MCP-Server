## Summary
Gary Marcus argues that the architectural choices behind o3 and Grok 4 — which blend neural networks with symbolic reasoning components — vindicate the neurosymbolic AI paradigm he has advocated for three decades. This matters for data leaders because it signals that reliable AI reasoning at enterprise scale likely requires structured, explicit knowledge representation alongside statistical models, not pure deep learning alone. Data governance strategies that assume LLMs will eventually self-correct through scale alone may need to be reconsidered in favor of hybrid architectures with more auditable reasoning chains.

## Key Excerpts
> Neural networks excel at pattern recognition from data, while symbolic systems handle explicit logic and causal reasoning — neither approach is sufficient alone for robust AI.
> Decades of AI funding flowed almost exclusively toward pure connectionist deep learning, creating an intellectual monoculture that suppressed exploration of hybrid alternatives.
> The latest frontier models appear to be quietly incorporating symbolic components, suggesting that architectural pluralism — not scaling alone — drives meaningful reasoning improvements.
