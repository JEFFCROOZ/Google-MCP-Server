## Summary
Stephen Wolfram provides a detailed technical explanation of how ChatGPT works by iteratively predicting the most probable next token given prior context, trained on vast human-written text. For data leaders, understanding this mechanism is critical for setting realistic expectations about LLM reliability, managing AI explainability obligations, and recognizing that model outputs are probabilistic continuations rather than reasoned conclusions—with direct implications for governance frameworks around AI-generated content.

## Key Excerpts
> ChatGPT does not reason or retrieve facts; it statistically continues text based on patterns learned from billions of human-written documents.
> Introducing controlled randomness via a "temperature" parameter improves output quality, but this voodoo-like tuning lacks formal theoretical justification.
> LLMs match meaning rather than literal text, making their behavior powerful but difficult to audit or predict with precision.
