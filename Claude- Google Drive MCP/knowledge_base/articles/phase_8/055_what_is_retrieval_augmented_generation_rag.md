## Summary
Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) is an AI architecture that enhances large language models by dynamically retrieving relevant external knowledge at inference time, rather than relying solely on static training data. For data leaders, RAG is strategically significant because it makes the quality, structure, and governance of an organization's internal knowledge assets directly determinative of AI output quality — transforming data management from a back-office function into a frontline AI capability.

## Key Excerpts
> RAG grounds LLM responses in verified, up-to-date organizational knowledge, reducing hallucinations and making AI outputs more trustworthy and auditable.
> The effectiveness of a RAG system depends heavily on how well-organized, indexed, and governed the underlying knowledge repositories are.
> By connecting LLMs to proprietary data sources, RAG enables enterprises to build AI systems that reflect institutional knowledge without expensive model retraining.
