## Summary
China's State Council released its "AI Plus" policy, a strategic roadmap targeting 70% AI agent adoption by 2027, 90% by 2030, and full "intelligent society" integration by 2035, framing AI as the successor to the "Internet Plus" era. For data leaders, this signals a state-directed acceleration of AI infrastructure at national scale, with explicit mandates across agriculture, industry, governance, and public services that will reshape data ecosystems, competitive dynamics, and AI talent markets globally.

## Key Excerpts
> Where "Internet Plus" connected people to services through information, "AI Plus" adds cognitive capability—shifting from information diffusion to active knowledge creation and application.
> By 2030, AI adoption at 90% penetration is expected to replace real estate and the internet as China's primary economic growth engine, with the intelligent economy becoming central to national prosperity.
> The policy explicitly addresses AI-driven job displacement, mandating large-scale reskilling programs and redirecting AI innovation toward job-creating rather than purely job-eliminating outcomes.
