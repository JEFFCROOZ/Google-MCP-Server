## Summary
Tristan Handy argues that AI will fundamentally transform data engineering between 2024 and 2028, automating a significant portion of routine tasks—from pipeline creation and testing to incident monitoring—potentially delivering 50–80% efficiency gains. For data leaders, this shift demands rethinking team structure, hiring profiles, and technology choices: standardized frameworks like dbt and Airbyte become strategic assets because AI performs dramatically better on consistent, well-documented codebases than on heterogeneous ones.

## Key Excerpts
> AI automates tasks, not jobs—data engineers will likely do more strategic work, but routine pipeline authoring, testing, and incident resolution are highly addressable by current AI systems.
> Codebases that are concise, consistent, and built on well-documented open frameworks give AI the greatest leverage, making standardization a competitive advantage in an AI-driven environment.
> Jevons paradox applies: efficiency gains from AI will expand the total demand for data engineering work, likely benefiting both engineers' compensation and companies' data ROI.
