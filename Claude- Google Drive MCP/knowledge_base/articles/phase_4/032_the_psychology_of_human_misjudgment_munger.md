## Summary
Charlie Munger's revised magnum opus systematically catalogs 25 cognitive biases and psychological tendencies that cause human misjudgment, with particular emphasis on how these biases compound into "lollapalooza" effects when combined. For data leaders, this framework is essential for understanding why stakeholders resist data-driven decisions, why teams make systematic analytical errors, and how to design governance structures that account for predictable irrationality rather than assuming rational actors.

## Key Excerpts
> Munger identifies 25 standard causes of human misjudgment, arguing that recognizing these patterns is critical to understanding both rational and irrational behavior in organizations.
> The most dangerous misjudgments occur when multiple cognitive biases reinforce each other simultaneously, creating outsized "lollapalooza" effects far greater than any single bias alone.
> Munger chose to publish despite risk of error, modeling that intellectual honesty and practical utility should outweigh reputational caution — a principle relevant to how data leaders communicate uncertainty.
