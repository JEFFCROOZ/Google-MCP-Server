## Summary
The article examines how behavioral scientists and designers deliberately engineer app features—variable rewards, streaks, notifications—to maximize user engagement and compulsive usage patterns. For data leaders, this matters because the same psychological principles used to hook users can inform how internal data products and dashboards are designed to drive adoption, while also raising ethical questions about manipulative design in data-driven consumer products that organizations may be building or enabling.

## Key Excerpts
> Behavioral scientists apply psychological research on reward loops and habit formation to make digital products feel irresistible, often prioritizing engagement metrics over user wellbeing.
> Variable reward schedules—unpredictable payoffs similar to slot machines—are among the most powerful mechanisms used to sustain compulsive app interaction.
> The discipline of "behavior design" treats human attention and decision-making as engineerable systems, with data on user actions continuously refined to increase retention and time-on-platform.
