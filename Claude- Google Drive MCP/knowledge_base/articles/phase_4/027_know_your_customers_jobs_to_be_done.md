## Summary
The article argues that innovation fails not from lack of effort but from misunderstanding *why* customers make choices — specifically, what "job" they are hiring a product or service to do. For data leaders, this framework reframes how to prioritize analytics investments: rather than building dashboards around available data, teams should identify the functional, emotional, and social jobs stakeholders need done, ensuring data products are designed around real decision needs rather than assumed ones.

## Key Excerpts
> Despite innovation being a near-universal strategic priority, the vast majority of initiatives fall short because organizations misread what customers actually need.
> Understanding the "job to be done" means identifying the specific progress a customer is trying to make in a given circumstance, not just their demographic profile.
> Companies that align products with genuine customer jobs — rather than product features or data they happen to have — consistently achieve more durable market success.
