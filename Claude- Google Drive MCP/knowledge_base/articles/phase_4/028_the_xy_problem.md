## Summary
The XY Problem describes a communication failure where someone asks for help with their attempted solution (Y) rather than their actual underlying need (X), wasting significant time for both parties. For data leaders, this pattern is endemic in analytics requests, debugging sessions, and stakeholder interactions—where teams solve the wrong problem precisely because the real requirement was never surfaced. Recognizing and preempting XY situations requires building a culture of problem framing before solution seeking.

## Key Excerpts
> People get trapped by their own assumed solution, losing the ability to step back and articulate the original problem they actually need to solve.
> When seeking help, always provide context about the broader goal alongside any specific technical question or attempted approach you're pursuing.
> If your diagnosis of the problem were correct, you likely wouldn't need help—so treat your framing as a hypothesis, not a fact.
