## Summary
Captology—the study of computers as persuasive technologies—centers on the Fogg Behavior Model, which holds that behavior occurs only when motivation, ability, and a trigger converge simultaneously. For data leaders, this model provides a rigorous framework for designing data adoption strategies, dashboards, and behavioral nudges that drive users toward desired actions rather than relying on training or mandates alone. Understanding why people fail to act (insufficient motivation, too much friction, or absent triggers) helps data teams diagnose adoption failures with precision.

## Key Excerpts
> Behavior change requires three simultaneous conditions: a person must be motivated, must have the ability to act, and must receive a timely trigger.
> Reducing friction—making the desired behavior easier—is often more effective than increasing motivation when driving user adoption of tools or processes.
> Persuasive design is not manipulation but intentional alignment of system prompts and user readiness to produce targeted, beneficial behaviors at scale.
