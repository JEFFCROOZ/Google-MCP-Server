## Summary
Drawing on a declassified WWII CIA sabotage manual, this article maps timeless organizational dysfunction tactics—bureaucratic delays, committee bloat, vanity metrics—onto modern software and data team contexts. For data leaders, it serves as a diagnostic framework: the patterns described (platform team understaffing, arbitrary system fragmentation, metric manipulation) are precisely the failure modes that undermine data platform reliability, trustworthiness, and delivery velocity.

## Key Excerpts
> Centralizing everything under an understaffed platform team while blocking others from building independently is a reliable way to create bottlenecks and stall delivery.
> Replacing meaningful business metrics with noisy vanity metrics, or dismissing valid ones on academic grounds, systematically disconnects teams from real outcomes.
> Linking compensation to headcount and title incentivizes organizational bloat, misaligned priorities, and the hoarding of resources over actual productivity.
