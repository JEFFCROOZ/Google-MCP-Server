## Summary
"Strong Opinions, Weakly Held" advocates forming confident, well-reasoned positions quickly while remaining genuinely open to revising them when confronted with new evidence — avoiding both paralysis and stubborn dogmatism. For data leaders, this framework is directly applicable to decision-making under uncertainty: whether prioritizing data initiatives, selecting tools, or setting governance policies, leaders must act decisively on current best thinking while building cultures that reward updating beliefs over defending positions.

## Key Excerpts
> Form bold, committed hypotheses based on available evidence, but treat them as provisional — ready to be discarded the moment better information arrives.
> The danger in data leadership is not taking a wrong position, but clinging to it after the evidence has shifted against you.
> Epistemic flexibility — the ability to hold conviction and humility simultaneously — is a core leadership competency, not a sign of weakness or indecision.
