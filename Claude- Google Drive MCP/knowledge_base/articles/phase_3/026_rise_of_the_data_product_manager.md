## Summary
The article argues that a distinct "Data Product Manager" role is emerging to bridge the gap between data teams and business stakeholders, treating data assets as products with defined owners, users, and value propositions. This matters for data leaders because it signals an organizational design shift—moving data from a support function toward a product-oriented operating model that demands clearer accountability, prioritization, and ROI measurement for data investments.

## Key Excerpts
> Data Product Managers own the strategy and roadmap for data assets, ensuring data teams build what stakeholders actually need rather than what seems technically interesting.
> Treating data as a product requires someone accountable for its quality, usability, and adoption—a gap traditional data engineering or analytics roles were never designed to fill.
> The rise of this role reflects growing organizational maturity, where data governance and value delivery must be managed with the same rigor applied to software products.
