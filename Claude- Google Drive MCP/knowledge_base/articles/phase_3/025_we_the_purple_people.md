## Summary
The article argues that "analytics engineering" as a label creates a false dichotomy between analytics and engineering, trapping practitioners in debates about technical credibility rather than focusing on impact. The author proposes the "purple people" metaphor—those who bridge deep business context (red) with technical expertise (blue)—as a more empowering framework for data role identity. For data leaders, this reframing has direct implications for how data teams are structured, hired, and positioned within organizations.

## Key Excerpts
> The analytics engineer label forces constant re-justification of technical credibility, when in reality all data work—SQL, LookML, forecasting—is inherently technical.
> Good analytics is measured by the impact of insight, not methodological complexity; the goal is ingenuity—abstracting the automatable to help many people at once.
> "Purple people" bridge business domain expertise and technical breadth, enabling translation between business stakeholders and engineering, making them uniquely valuable in modern data organizations.
