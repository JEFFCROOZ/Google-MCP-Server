## Summary
The article argues that BI success depends on "purple people" — professionals who blend business and technology expertise — because neither pure technologists nor pure business analysts alone can bridge the cultural and linguistic divide between IT and business departments. For data leaders, this has direct implications for team composition and hiring strategy: recruiting experienced professionals who have genuinely worked on both sides (e.g., a financial analyst who joins the BI team) creates more effective translation of business needs into data solutions than relying on junior generalists.

## Key Excerpts
> Purple people are neither pure technologists nor pure business professionals, but experienced practitioners who have credibly worked in both domains and can bridge the cultural divide between them.
> The most effective purple people are "switch hitters" — those with a solid reputation in either IT or business who then cross over, bringing real-world context that pure translators lack.
> BI directors should actively recruit business professionals from across departments to serve as embedded liaisons, forming the connective tissue that sustains alignment between the BI team and business stakeholders.
