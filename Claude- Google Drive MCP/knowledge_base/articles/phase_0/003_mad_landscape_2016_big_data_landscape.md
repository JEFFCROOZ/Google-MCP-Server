## Summary
The 2016 MAD (Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence & Data) Landscape maps the rapidly expanding ecosystem of big data tools, platforms, and vendors, illustrating how the market has fragmented across infrastructure, analytics, and application layers. For data leaders, this landscape serves as a strategic reference for vendor evaluation, technology stack decisions, and understanding where consolidation or commoditization is occurring in the data market.

## Key Excerpts
> The big data ecosystem had grown dramatically by 2016, with hundreds of vendors competing across overlapping categories, making technology selection increasingly complex for organizations.
> Infrastructure, analytics, and application layers were maturing at different rates, requiring data leaders to think carefully about build-versus-buy decisions at each tier.
> Market consolidation was beginning to emerge around dominant platforms, signaling that early-stage fragmentation would give way to fewer, more integrated solutions over time.
