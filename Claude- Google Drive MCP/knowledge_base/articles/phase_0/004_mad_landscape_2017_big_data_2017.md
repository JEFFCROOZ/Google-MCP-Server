## Summary
The 2017 MAD (Machine learning, Artificial intelligence, and Data) Landscape maps the rapidly expanding ecosystem of tools and vendors across the big data stack, from infrastructure to analytics to AI applications. For data leaders, this landscape signals that the data tooling market had reached a point of significant fragmentation and maturity, requiring intentional architectural choices and vendor evaluation frameworks rather than ad-hoc adoption.

## Key Excerpts
> The big data ecosystem had grown dramatically complex by 2017, with hundreds of vendors competing across overlapping categories, making coherent data strategy increasingly difficult.
> Open source technologies continued to accelerate commoditization of core infrastructure, shifting competitive differentiation toward higher-level analytics and AI capabilities.
> Consolidation pressures were emerging as enterprises struggled to integrate fragmented point solutions into coherent, governed data platforms.
