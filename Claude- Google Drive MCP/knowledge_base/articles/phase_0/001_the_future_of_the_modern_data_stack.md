## Summary
Tristan Handy traces the evolution of the modern data stack through three phases—a 2012–2016 Cambrian explosion catalyzed by cloud-native MPP databases, a 2016–2020 deployment/maturation period, and an anticipated second wave of innovation from 2020–2025. The article argues that the stack has reached a functional plateau despite incremental improvements, and that understanding these innovation cycles is critical for data leaders making long-term infrastructure and team investment decisions.

## Key Excerpts
> Amazon Redshift's 2012 launch democratized analytical infrastructure by slashing costs from $100K+/year to $160/month, catalyzing an entire ecosystem of modern data tooling.
> Despite years of incremental improvements, core user experiences across ingestion, warehousing, transformation, and BI tools have remained largely unchanged since 2016—signaling maturity, not momentum.
> A data leader's tooling choices directly shape team capabilities; understanding where the stack is in its innovation cycle determines when transformative productivity gains are actually available.
