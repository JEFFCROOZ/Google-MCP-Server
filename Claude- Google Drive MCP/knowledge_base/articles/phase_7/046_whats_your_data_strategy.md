## Summary
This HBR article argues that companies need two complementary data strategies—offensive (value creation) and defensive (risk/compliance management)—and that the critical challenge is balancing both. It matters for data leaders because most organizations are severely underutilizing their data assets while simultaneously failing to protect them, signaling a foundational gap in data governance maturity.

## Key Excerpts
> Less than half of structured organizational data is actively used in decisions, and under 1% of unstructured data is ever analyzed, revealing massive untapped value.
> Over 70% of employees have access to data they shouldn't, highlighting that governance failures are the norm, not the exception, in most organizations.
> Analysts waste 80% of their time just finding and preparing data, meaning poor data infrastructure directly undermines the productivity of data talent.
