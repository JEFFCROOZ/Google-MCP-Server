## Summary
Big Data didn't die but underwent a metamorphosis: its core innovations (distributed file systems, fault-tolerant processing, scalable compute) were absorbed into mainstream cloud infrastructure like S3 and Spark, becoming invisible utilities rather than differentiating capabilities. For data leaders, this evolution signals that today's cutting-edge paradigms (streaming, data mesh) will similarly normalize, making architectural foresight and adaptability more valuable than chasing terminology trends.

## Key Excerpts
> The descent from Big Data hype forced organizations to prove demonstrable value, shifting focus from buzzword adoption to measurable outcomes—a discipline data leaders must institutionalize.
> HDFS evolved into S3, MapReduce into Spark and SQL, showing that big data tools matured by abstracting complexity and converging toward familiar, accessible interfaces.
> Hadoop's design philosophy of treating failures as routine rather than exceptional became the foundational expectation for all modern cloud-scale services and architectures.
