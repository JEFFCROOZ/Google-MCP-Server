## Summary
This 2020 a16z report maps the emerging architecture of modern data infrastructure by synthesizing interviews with 20+ practitioners, distinguishing between analytic systems (built around data warehouses) and operational/ML systems (built around data lakes), while documenting their convergence. For data leaders, it provides a concrete reference architecture and common vocabulary at a moment when data infrastructure spending hit $66B and CDO adoption was accelerating—offering a strategic framework for stack decisions and vendor evaluation.

## Key Excerpts
> Data infrastructure now serves two distinct purposes: enabling better business decisions through analytics, and powering data-driven products via machine learning—each requiring fundamentally different architectural foundations.
> Modern data warehouses and data lakes are converging, both adopting commodity storage, horizontal scaling, and semi-structured data support, blurring previously rigid architectural boundaries.
> Chief Data Officer adoption grew from 12% to 60% of Fortune 1000 companies between 2012 and 2020, with data-mature organizations meaningfully outperforming peers on growth and profitability.
