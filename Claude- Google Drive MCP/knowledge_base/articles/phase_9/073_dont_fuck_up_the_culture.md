## Summary
Brian Chesky argues that culture is a company's most fragile and important asset — once lost, it is nearly impossible to recover, making its preservation a primary leadership responsibility. For data leaders, this is a direct mandate: the norms, values, and behaviors embedded in a data team (around quality, transparency, accountability, and ethical use of data) must be actively cultivated and protected, not left to drift as organizations scale.

## Key Excerpts
> Culture is not a passive byproduct of hiring good people — it requires deliberate, ongoing reinforcement from leadership at every level of the organization.
> The greatest threat to culture is scaling too fast without maintaining the intentional values that defined the organization in its early, most effective stages.
> A strong culture acts as a decision-making framework, enabling teams to act autonomously while remaining aligned — critical for distributed data organizations.
