## Summary
Tristan Handy argues that the post-pandemic macroeconomic environment—cheap capital, inflated VC valuations, and now rising interest rates—has directly shaped the modern data tooling landscape, and that an impending pullback will force data teams to mature their financial discipline. For data leaders, this means the era of unchecked cloud spend and tool proliferation is ending, requiring deliberate cost attribution, stack rationalization, and clearer ROI justification across the entire data infrastructure.

## Key Excerpts
> The low-interest-rate environment flooded VC into data tooling, accelerating innovation but also creating froth, brain drain, and hype that may not survive an economic correction.
> Data leaders must now rigorously track and attribute cloud spend across the modern data stack, eliminating unused models, pipelines, and dashboard refreshes to demonstrate fiscal maturity.
> Macroeconomic contraction may compress both VC-funded tool development and enterprise data budgets, making cost governance a strategic priority rather than a deferred task.
