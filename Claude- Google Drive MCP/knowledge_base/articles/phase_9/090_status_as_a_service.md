## Summary
Eugene Wei argues that social networks are best understood as "Status as a Service" (StaaS) businesses — platforms that generate and distribute social capital as their core product, not just software utility. This framing matters for data leaders because it reframes engagement metrics: user behavior on social platforms is driven by status-seeking incentives, meaning standard product analytics must account for social capital dynamics to accurately interpret growth, retention, and network effects.

## Key Excerpts
> Social capital often precedes financial capital as a leading indicator, making its dynamics critical to understanding why social networks grow, stall, or collapse.
> People instinctively seek the most efficient path to maximizing social capital, which shapes user behavior in ways that purely functional or economic models fail to predict.
> Analyzing social networks like SaaS businesses — but where the "service" is status rather than software — unlocks a more rigorous framework for understanding network effects and platform value.
