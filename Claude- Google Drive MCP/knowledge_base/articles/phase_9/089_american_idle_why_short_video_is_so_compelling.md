## Summary
Eugene Wei argues that TikTok's dominance stems from a strong network effect of creativity—each new user expands the creative toolkit for all users through built-in remixing, dueting, and low-barrier editing features. For data leaders, this illustrates how platform design choices (reducing friction, enabling composability) can compound network value in ways that traditional engagement metrics or GDP figures fail to capture, demanding richer frameworks for measuring creative productivity and user-generated value.

## Key Excerpts
> TikTok creates a powerful creative network effect where every additional user makes every other user more creative through explicit remixing and duet features.
> Lowering technical barriers—like abstracting complex video editing into one-tap filters—drives measurable productivity gains that conventional economic metrics like GDP per capita cannot capture.
> Emergent creativity at scale only requires a handful of innovators; the platform's role is to distribute and remix their innovations instantly across the entire user base.
