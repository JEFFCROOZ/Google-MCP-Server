## Summary
Steve Yegge argues that software development is rapidly evolving through six overlapping waves—from traditional coding to agentic and fleet-based AI systems—where individual developers will soon manage clusters of AI coding agents rather than writing code manually. For data leaders, this signals a fundamental shift in how data engineering and analytics teams will be structured and scaled, with AI agents handling implementation work while human judgment focuses on architecture, oversight, and business alignment.

## Key Excerpts
> AI coding is progressing through distinct phases—chat, agents, agent clusters, fleet—each arriving faster than predicted, with agentic coding set to eclipse chat-based approaches by mid-2025.
> Future developers will function as managers of AI agent teams, simultaneously directing bug fixes, new features, and architectural migrations through supervised automation.
> The distinction between "vibe coding" and serious engineering is collapsing—AI-driven development will apply to production systems, not just prototypes, requiring deliberate human oversight rather than disengagement.
