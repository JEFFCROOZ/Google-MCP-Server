## Summary
Data teams are experiencing a "Jevons Paradox" where lower storage costs have driven greater data consumption, making cost management harder even as unit economics improve. The article argues that data teams must shift from reactive service providers to proactive value creators by embedding themselves in product delivery, tying work to ARR/OKRs, and raising quality standards—transforming from cost centers into measurable profit drivers. For data leaders, this reframing requires both cultural change and operational discipline, including formalized analytics development lifecycle practices and standardized metric definitions.

## Key Excerpts
> Transitioning from background support to customer-facing product delivery increases data team visibility, accountability, and SLAs—but also unlocks greater resources and organizational recognition.
> Associating all incoming data work with ARR or OKRs allows teams to directly demonstrate business impact and make principled decisions about where to invest limited resources.
> Embedding data teams within product teams makes ROI justification self-evident, reducing the risk that platform investments face hostile quarterly cost scrutiny.
