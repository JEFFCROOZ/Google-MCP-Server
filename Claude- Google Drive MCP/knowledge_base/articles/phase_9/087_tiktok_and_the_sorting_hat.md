## Summary
Eugene Wei argues that TikTok's breakthrough in culturally foreign markets was enabled by a highly responsive machine learning recommendation algorithm that effectively bypassed the need for deep cultural understanding — a capability he calls "abstracting culture." This matters for data leaders because it demonstrates that sufficiently accurate, data-driven personalization systems can substitute for domain or cultural expertise, reframing where algorithmic investment creates strategic competitive advantage.

## Key Excerpts
> A sufficiently accurate and responsive machine learning algorithm can pierce cultural barriers that have historically blocked foreign companies from entering new markets.
> TikTok's success challenges the assumption that product-market fit requires human cultural knowledge; recommendation systems can substitute for that understanding at scale.
> The rise of algorithm-driven content platforms suggests that data infrastructure and ML capability, not cultural intuition, may be the primary moat in cross-border consumer tech competition.
