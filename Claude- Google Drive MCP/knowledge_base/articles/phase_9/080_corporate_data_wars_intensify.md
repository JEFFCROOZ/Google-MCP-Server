## Summary
Corporate competition is increasingly being fought on the terrain of proprietary data assets, with organizations racing to acquire, secure, and leverage unique datasets as sources of competitive differentiation. For data leaders, this dynamic elevates the strategic importance of data governance frameworks that not only protect data quality and access but also position the organization to monetize and defend its data advantage against competitors and potential regulatory scrutiny.

## Key Excerpts
> Companies that control proprietary, hard-to-replicate datasets are gaining durable competitive moats, making data acquisition and curation a core corporate strategy priority.
> Data leadership is shifting from an operational function to a boardroom concern, as executives recognize that data assets directly influence market valuation and competitive positioning.
> Organizations without clear data ownership, lineage, and governance risk losing ground to rivals who can move faster and more confidently with their data investments.
