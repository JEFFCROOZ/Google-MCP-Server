## Summary
Benn Stancil applies a "tenth man" contrarian framework to interrogate how Snowflake—despite its dominant market position and strong financials—could plausibly fail, using the data industry's post-pandemic boom-and-bust arc as a cautionary backdrop. For data leaders, this matters because uncritical consensus around incumbent platforms creates strategic blind spots, and understanding failure modes helps inform more resilient architecture and vendor diversification decisions.

## Key Excerpts
> Snowflake's pricing complaints are largely driven by rising demand, not artificial price hikes—meaning cost concerns alone are unlikely to unseat it from its dominant position.
> The data industry's 2021–2022 bubble mirrored broader tech excess, with valuations and fundraising detached from fundamentals, leaving many companies structurally fragile when markets corrected.
> Even seemingly inevitable winners like Zoom and Slack lost their dominance quickly, suggesting that confident consensus around Snowflake's future should be stress-tested, not accepted as given.
