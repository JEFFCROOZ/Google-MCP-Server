## Summary
Eugene Wei argues that TikTok's algorithmic advantage stems not from mysterious proprietary code, but from deliberate product design choices that make user behavior maximally "legible" to its recommendation system. For data leaders, this reframes algorithm strategy: the critical question is not only how sophisticated your ML models are, but whether your product architecture generates the right signals at sufficient volume and clarity to train them effectively.

## Key Excerpts
> TikTok's design allows its algorithm to "see" user preferences with precision — product structure and data signal quality are as decisive as the algorithm itself.
> Breakthroughs in machine learning often come from dramatically increasing training data volume, not from fundamentally new algorithms — scale and signal matter more than code complexity.
> The failure mode of over-simplifying complex systems for legibility — Scott's "Seeing Like a State" — applies equally to social platforms imposing false order on user behavior.
