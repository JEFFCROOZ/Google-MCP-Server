## Summary
"AI Eats the World" argues that AI is not merely a technological upgrade but a fundamental restructuring of industries, culture, and organizational operating models—echoing Marc Andreessen's "software eats the world" thesis but at greater speed and scope. For data leaders, this framing is critical: it signals that data governance frameworks, team structures, and decision-making processes must be redesigned for AI-native environments rather than retrofitted from legacy data practices. Leaders who treat AI as a tool rather than a transformational force risk being institutionally outpaced by competitors who are rebuilding around AI from the ground up.

## Key Excerpts
> AI is not a feature to be added to existing workflows but a foundational shift that rewrites how organizations create value, compete, and structure teams.
> Cultural adaptation to AI is as consequential as technical adoption — organizations that resist behavioral change will fail regardless of their technology investments.
> Data leaders must move beyond managing data pipelines to orchestrating AI-ready data ecosystems that enable autonomous decision-making at scale.
