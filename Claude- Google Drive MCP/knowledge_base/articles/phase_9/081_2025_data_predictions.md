## Summary
Tristan Handy's decade-in-review traces the data industry's evolution from the data science era through the modern data stack boom, the 2023 reckoning, and 2024's stabilization—concluding that AI is amplifying rather than replacing structured data needs, Iceberg has won the open table format battle, and enterprise consolidation is accelerating. For data leaders, this matters because it reframes AI as a catalyst for increased data infrastructure investment rather than a disruption, while signaling that architectural decisions around open formats and platform consolidation are now boardroom-level conversations.

## Key Excerpts
> AI in 2024 shifted from a perceived threat to a growth driver for data pipelines, with EL tools and cloud platforms accelerating as AI initiatives demanded more structured data.
> Apache Iceberg emerged as the clear winner in open table formats after both Snowflake and Databricks CEOs publicly committed, driving rapid executive-level interest across the enterprise.
> After the 2023 macro pullback forced short-term thinking, 2024 saw enterprise CIOs and CDOs return to long-term strategic data planning, though venture-backed SMB-focused data companies continued to struggle.
