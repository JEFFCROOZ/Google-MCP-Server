## Summary
The data industry is experiencing a renewed wave of consolidation and investment (Salesforce-Informatica, Databricks-Neon, Snowflake's product expansions), echoing the 2022 boom after a prolonged downturn. Stancil argues that the original "modern data stack" was fundamentally built for analytics and reporting, and that industry leaders like dbt's Tristan Handy are now advocating for reframing the entire ecosystem as an "analytics stack" to better reflect its actual purpose and business value. For data leaders, this reframing has strategic implications for how teams justify budgets, organize tooling decisions, and position data functions within the broader enterprise.

## Key Excerpts
> The modern data stack was always primarily built for reporting and analysis, not operational data management — a foundational truth obscured by years of infrastructure-focused branding.
> Major platforms like Snowflake are aggressively expanding into adjacent categories including ETL, BI, observability, and data modeling, intensifying competitive pressure on specialized vendors.
> Rebranding the ecosystem as the "analytics stack" grounds data tooling in its true business purpose — delivering analytics value — rather than celebrating its cloud-era technical origins.
