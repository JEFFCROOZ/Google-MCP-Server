## Summary
Through a parable format, the article diagnoses the dysfunction that emerges when data teams operate in organizationally siloed, low-maturity environments: fragmented data ownership, ungoverned analytical work scattered across functions, and a disconnect between data team outputs and production deployment. It matters for data leaders because it illustrates how cultural and structural barriers—not technical ones—are the primary blockers to data value delivery, and how a new data leader must diagnose these dynamics before building or scaling a team.

## Key Excerpts
> Fragmented data ownership across functions means no single team has accountability for consistent metrics, creating conflicting narratives and ungoverned analytical work.
> Data teams build sophisticated models that never reach production because cross-functional prioritization failures and lack of engineering partnership strand their work in notebooks.
> Critical business processes driven by brittle spreadsheet models represent hidden data governance risk, especially when those models determine vendor payments and daily operational priorities.
