## Summary
Monica Rogati argues that organizations must satisfy foundational data needs—collection, storage, pipelines, and analytics—before AI and machine learning can deliver value, mirroring Maslow's hierarchy of needs. This framework is critical for data leaders because it diagnoses why AI initiatives fail: companies attempt to build sophisticated models atop broken or absent data infrastructure, skipping essential prerequisites. It provides a prioritization roadmap for data investment decisions and organizational readiness assessments.

## Key Excerpts
> Attempting to implement AI without solid data infrastructure is like trying to self-actualize without first having food and shelter—the foundation must come first.
> Most companies overestimate their AI readiness; reliable data collection, storage, and transformation pipelines must be mature before machine learning adds meaningful value.
> Data leaders should resist pressure to deploy AI prematurely, and instead invest in the unglamorous but essential lower layers of the data stack.
