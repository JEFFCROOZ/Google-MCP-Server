## Summary
The article argues that data should be treated not just as a product, but specifically as a *software* product, demanding the same rigor in developer experience, product ownership, and architectural patterns applied in software engineering. It raises foundational questions data leaders must resolve—what a data product actually is, when to apply microservices patterns, and how team roles map to software product teams—to build a coherent, scalable data organization. Resolving these questions is critical for data governance because vague definitions of "data product" lead to misaligned ownership, inconsistent quality, and poor end-user trust.

## Key Excerpts
> The modern data experience is broken for both data producers and consumers, often manifesting as conflicting metrics, stale data, and unreliable reports reaching executives.
> Data products can take at least three forms—internal consulting service, self-serve SaaS-style application, or insight delivery—each implying different ownership, measurement, and governance models.
> Treating data science outputs as "quasi-mystical arts" is unsustainable; turning them into transparent, trustworthy systems is a core challenge for the analytics engineering discipline.
