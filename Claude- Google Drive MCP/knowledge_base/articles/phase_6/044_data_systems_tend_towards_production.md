## Summary
Data systems, regardless of their original intent, inevitably migrate toward production use as stakeholders begin relying on their outputs for decisions. This phenomenon has significant implications for data leaders, who must design governance, quality controls, and operational standards into data systems from the outset rather than retrofitting them after adoption. Treating "experimental" or "exploratory" data work as permanently non-production is a dangerous assumption that leads to fragile infrastructure supporting critical business decisions.

## Key Excerpts
> Data systems that start as ad hoc experiments frequently become load-bearing infrastructure before proper engineering standards or governance controls are applied.
> The gap between how data engineers classify a system and how business users actually depend on it creates significant organizational and technical risk.
> Data leaders should apply production-grade thinking — reliability, documentation, and ownership — to data products early, anticipating inevitable stakeholder reliance.
