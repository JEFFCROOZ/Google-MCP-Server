## Summary
DataOps applies agile and DevOps principles to data management, emphasizing automation, self-service access, and small iterative changes to help teams produce reliable, high-quality data products at scale. For data leaders, it reframes the data engineering role from a request-fulfillment bottleneck into an enablement function — shifting responsibility and capability to analysts and scientists directly. The principles offer a governance model that balances speed with reliability through autonomation, reproducibility, and continuous quality monitoring.

## Key Excerpts
> Self-service data platforms that let analysts build and reuse their own transformations are essential; making everyone both a producer and consumer of data unlocks far greater organizational capability.
> Autonomation — starting with manual processes to understand the problem before automating — prevents premature abstractions and ensures data pipelines solve the right problems reliably.
> Batching changes in small increments with automated quality checks and built-in rollback mechanisms reduces risk and eliminates the bureaucratic change-freeze cycles common in traditional data governance.
