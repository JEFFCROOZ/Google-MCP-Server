## Summary
Tristan Handy argues that while software engineering best practices (version control, testing, CI/CD) have been successfully applied to data transformation, they have not been extended to the full analytics stack—ingestion, notebooks, dashboards, and incident management—leaving most organizations with impaired data trust and slow decision velocity. The Analytics Development Lifecycle (ADLC) is proposed as an end-to-end workflow framework to close this gap. For data leaders, this reframes data maturity as an organization-wide operational discipline, not merely a tooling or transformation problem.

## Key Excerpts
> Despite a decade of progress, mature analytics principles remain absent from most of the stack beyond transformation, resulting in persistent problems of low velocity, inaccuracy, and eroded trust.
> A mature analytics workflow must simultaneously achieve data scale, collaboration scale, correctness, auditability, and governance—not trade one against another as most teams currently do.
> The ADLC positions analytics maturity as an end-to-end effort, requiring workflow discipline from ingestion through orchestration, observability, discovery, and analysis layers.
